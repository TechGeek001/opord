    Concept Sketch / Operational Graphics

    The Concept Sketch and Operational Graphics are created using an almost identical application, except that the Concept Sketch does not have a background image, and the Operational Graphics do not use tactical task symbols such as suppress or destroy. Unless specifically noted, the help content below applies to both applications.

    Getting an Image from Google Maps

    To take an image off of Google Maps, we need to take, edit, and save a screenshot of the terrain in question. To do this on a Windows computer, follow the steps below:

    1. Navigate to Google Maps in your browser, and turn Satellite View on by clicking the Earth button in the bottom left corner. Find the are you want to take a screenshot of on the Maps program.

    2. Go to Start > All Programs > Accessories. If your computer has the Snipping Tool application, follow the instructions under Use the Snipping Tool (preferred). If your computer does NOT have the Snipping Tool application, follow the instructions under Use Microsoft Paint.

    2a. Use the Snipping Tool (preferred) to take the screenshot. Open the program and use the crosshair to drag a box around the area you want to select. Note that it is a good idea to capture more than just the area you want to use on your graphics. If you don't like the area you selected the first time, click "New" to try again. Once you are happy with your image, click the "Save" icon and store the image in a place you can get to easily (on your Desktop). Skip to Upload the image.

    2b. Use Microsoft Paint to take the screenshot. Press the Print Screen key on your keyboard. Open Microsoft Paint in Start > All Programs > Accessories. In the upper left corner of Paint, click the Paste button, or press Ctrl + V at the same time on your keyboard. Click the Select button and drag a box around the area of your screen that you want to use on your graphics. Note that it is a good idea to include more than just the area you want to use.

    In the upper left corner, click the Crop button, or press Ctrl + Shift + X at the same time on your keyboard. This will leave only the area you have selected. Click the menu button in the upper left corner and select Save As. Store your image in an easy to remember place (your Desktop, for example).

    3. Upload the image
